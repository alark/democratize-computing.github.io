Girl Tech Power is a code camp that teaches middle school and high school girls the basics of HTML, Python, App Inventor, and computer architecture to promote diversity aong programmers and introduce computer science at a younge age. We feel that girls feel intimidated in the tech field because they are not what you think of when you think of a "stereotypical" programmer. We want to break that stereotype.

Summer 2015

We led a 5 day long camp of 50 girls with workshops for HTML, Alice, Python, and Computer Architecture. We broke the girls up into two groups of high schoolers and middle schoolers so we can adjust the difficulty of the workshops accordingly. To cap off the week long camp, we took the girls on the trip to Facebook and introduced them to some female engineers and gave them a peek of what their lives in tech would look like. We also gave the girls time to take the knowledge their acquired during the workshops to create a project of their choice using Python, HTML, or Alice.

We saw great success with our first time hosting this camp and received amazing feedback from the parents inquiring about hosting weekend workshops and more code camps in the upcoming breaks. We will continue to expand our programs to help these girls explore the tech industry and develop skills to develop their own apps, websites, and programs.
