What is Girl Tech Power?

"While an introductiong to CS concepts at earlier ages encourages more diversity among scholars and practitioners in the field, there are not enough instructors prepared to guide our next generation of innovators." -Google

Girl Tech Power is a code camp that teaches middle school and high school girls the basics of HTML, Python, App Inventor, and computer architecture to promote diversity among programmers and introduce Computer Science at a young age. We feel that girls feel intimidated in the tech field because they are not what you think of when you think of a "stereotypical" programmer. We want to break that stereotype.

What does the camp look like?

The camp is held over 5 days in the summer and we host around 50 girls with workshops for HTML, Alice, Scratch, Python, and Computer Architecture. The middle and high school girls are split into two groups during lectures to provide the appropriate level of difficulty for grad level. To cap off the week long camp, we take the girls on the trip to a local Bay Area tech company and introduced them to some female engineers and gave them a peek of what their lives in tech would look like. We also gave the girls time to take the knowledge their acquired during the workshops to create a project of their choice using Python, HTML, Scratch, Alice, or AppInventor.
